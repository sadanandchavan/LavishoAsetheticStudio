Acne Treatment

Acne is a common skin problem in which the pores of your skin get blocked. Blackheads, whiteheads, and other kinds of pimples are caused by pore blockages. On your skin, pimples are lumps that occasionally hurt and are packed with pus.

What are the types of acne?

✓Fungal acne (pityrosporum folliculitis)

✓Cystic acne

✓Hormonal acne

✓Nodular acne

What are the symptoms of acne?

✓Pimples (pustules): Pus-filled bumps (papules).

✓Papules: Small, discolored bumps, often red to purple or darker than your natural skin tone.

✓Blackheads: Plugged pores with a black top.

✓Whiteheads: Plugged pores with a white top.

✓Nodules: Large lumps under your skin that are painful.

✓Cysts: Painful fluid-filled (pus) lumps under your skin.

Who treats acne?

Acne can be detected and tackled by a dermatologist or general physician. A dermatologist can assist you if you have stubborn acne that is not improving with the therapy.

How can dermatologists determine the cause of acne?

A dermatologist may identify acne just by looking at your pimples. A dermatologist will also note what forms of acne you're suffering from and where breakouts appear on your skin during the checkup. This assists your dermatologist to plan an efficient treatment strategy.

Perioral dermatitis is a different condition that is often confused for acne. It frequently produces acne-like outbreaks around the mouth.

A consultation with a board-certified dermatologist might be comforting. A board-certified dermatologist can diagnose you and establish a treatment plan suited to your specific requirements whether you have stubborn acne or another problem.

What methods do dermatologists use to treat acne?

Many factors influence the acne treatment examine which is best for you, including

What kind of acne you have, such as blackheads, pimples, etc.

The place of the acne on your skin

What medical treatments have you already tried?

Your age when the outbreaks started.

Whether you have black patches or scars from acne.

A healthy diet: According to certain research, what you eat might also help you have smoother skin. If you feel that what you're consuming is causing your breakouts, do some research.
